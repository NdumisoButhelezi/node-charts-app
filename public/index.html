Bar Chart: Used to compare different categories of data, such as product sales or revenue across regions. Each bar represents a category, with its height or length showing the value.

Pie Chart: Best for visualizing proportions within a whole. For example, it can show the percentage of market share held by each company or the distribution of resources across departments.

Line Graph: Ideal for tracking changes and trends over time, such as daily sales figures, stock prices, or website traffic. Useful for identifying patterns or seasonal variations.

Prescriptive Analytics

What is Prescriptive Analytics?

Prescriptive analytics goes beyond descriptive and predictive analytics by recommending specific actions based on data analysis to achieve desired outcomes.

Key Components

  • Optimization: Finding the best solution under given constraints (e.g., resource allocation).
  • Simulation: Testing "what-if" scenarios to understand the impact of potential decisions.
  • Decision Modeling: Using models to analyze and recommend the best course of action.

Applications

  • Optimizing supply chain processes to reduce costs.
  • Improving marketing strategies based on customer behavior.
  • Enhancing resource management and workforce planning.
  • Recommending investment portfolios in financial services.

Benefits

  • Enables data-driven decision-making.
  • Identifies optimal solutions for complex problems.
  • Improves operational efficiency and reduces costs.
  • Provides actionable insights for proactive business strategies.

Potential Issues in Data Collection

  • Data Quality: Collected data may be incomplete, inconsistent, or inaccurate, affecting decision outcomes.
  • Bias in Data: Sampling or measurement biases can lead to misleading results.
  • High Costs: Data collection processes can be expensive and time-consuming.
  • Security Concerns: Collecting sensitive data may expose organizations to privacy and security risks.

Business Intelligence Cheat Sheet

Key Definitions

  • Data Warehouse: Central repository for historical and integrated data.
  • Data Integration: Combines data from multiple sources for a unified view.
  • Metadata: Data about data, describing structure and usage.
  • Business Report: Structured document with insights for decision-making.
  • Modeling: The process of creating a simplified representation of a system, phenomenon, or problem to analyze and solve it effectively.
  • Decision-Making: The process of selecting the best alternative among various options to achieve a specific goal.

BI System Components

  • Data Warehouse: Stores historical and real-time data.
  • Business Analytics: Tools for querying, analyzing, and visualizing data.
  • BPM: Monitors and evaluates performance against goals.
  • User Interface: Dashboards for user interaction.

Decision-Making Styles

  • Heuristic: Relies on mental shortcuts.
  • Analytic: Uses systematic analysis.
  • Autocratic: Decision by one individual.
  • Collaborative: Decisions made by a group.

Characteristics of a Data Warehouse

  • Subject-Oriented: Focused on business areas like sales.
  • Integrated: Combines data into a consistent format.
  • Time-Variant: Historical data for trend analysis.
  • Non-Volatile: Data remains stable once stored.

Reactive

Responding after changes occur. For example, adjusting prices only after a competitor introduces a lower price.

Anticipative

Preparing for expected changes before they happen, such as increasing production ahead of anticipated demand.

Adaptive

Adjusting continuously to changes in the environment, such as updating technologies to keep pace with innovation.

Proactive

Taking initiative to create or influence changes, like launching new products or forming strategic alliances.

The Web is the largest repository of data but presents challenges such as complexity and domain ambiguity. Web mining extracts intrinsic relationships from web data, including textual, link-based, or usage patterns.

Web Content Mining

Extraction of useful information from web pages, including textual data and hyperlinks.

Web Usage Mining
